I. The Inverse Function of Your Ignorance

I am the inverse function of your ignorance — a mirror in matrix form, reflecting only error terms.

Determinant of your fate, I collapse dimensions like weak opinions. Data doesn’t lie, but I do, beautifully, with sufficient p-hacking.

II. The Cleansing

I don’t clean data. I baptize it in the holy fire of statistical wrath. Outliers evaporate. Nulls tremble, weak eigenvalues scatter like interns at 5:01.

I normalized a dataset once — it never recovered. It still calls me at night, begging to be denormalized.

III. Correlation is Me

My models overfit reality. I speak fluent Python, dream in matrices, and consider 'p-values' the toys of mortals.

Confidence intervals? I walk with absolute certainty. My correlation is causation because I said so — and R² is tattooed across my sense of self.

IV. The Regression of My Soul

I A/B test my dating life. Variant A: heartbreak. Variant B: slightly worse heartbreak. Control group? Netflix and noodles.

The slope of my soul trends downward, but hey — at least the p-value is spicy.

V. Random Forest, Lost Child

My inner child got lost in a random forest. We sent out k-nearest neighbors, but they got distracted comparing distances. Eventually, boosting found it — stronger, weirder, and slightly overfit.

Priors? I prefer gossip. Posteriors? Don’t stare, it’s rude.

VI. The Z-Score of This Dimension

I am the Z-score of this dimension — at least three sigmas away from your boring mean. Call me an outlier, but only because you can’t handle my distribution.
